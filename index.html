How To: Growth Strategies

We Value Your Business Growth Strategies

5 ways to develop crucial business relationships that last

By Simon T. Bailey
 – Contributing Writer,
Oct 1, 2015, 8:57am EDT

Relationships underpin businesses. Whether it is a strong relationship with its suppliers, customers, shareholders,or even the local government, building and maintaining successful relationships with them is what allows a firm to establish its integrity and trust, and grow overall.

However, due to the dynamic environment in which firms operate today, building relationships is no longer limited to making sales calls or having face-to-face meetings.

Complex organizational hierarchies, the presence of the online platform and multiple stakeholders, all have made it far more difficult than before.

Here, we will delve into some of the ways through which businesses can develop and sustain important business relationships over time.

1. Keep a lookout for key influencers

Before you seek to develop business relationships, it is important to know which people will be critical for the effort. Successful people who have a large following tend to have the most leverage in spreading awareness about your business or brand, and can add a lot of credibility to your business profile.

These are known as key influencers, and approaching them for building and sustaining business relationships can catapult your business to enormous success. You can identify successful and established influencers in your industry through social media such as Twitter and LinkedIn, and through your own personal contacts.

2. Find common interests

Common interests are what push us to befriend some people over others, and the same applies to building and sustaining business relationships. It is important to identify the interests of your key influencers and other business contacts.

You can find out what appeals to them and how you can work together with them on specific business projects. If you have an asset that can be of major use to your contacts, then approach them to discuss ways to maximize that value by working together.

3. Join online and offline conversations

It is essential for you to maintain your presence on both online and offline channels. You can only hope to find meaningful and relevant people for developing business relationships if you are visible on the particular platforms on which people communicate with others.

Therefore, be sure to attend important conferences and exhibitions pertaining to your industry or expertise, and participate in conversations on Twitter through which you can have comfortable dialogue with important people.

4. Provide valuable information

It is human nature for people to be attracted to those who can appeal to their interests. Naturally, businesses that provide valuable information through e-mails, blogs, websites, e-books and magazines can draw a lot of followers.

Therefore, it's crucial that you create a content and marketing strategy that relates exclusively to each of your stakeholder’s interests. You can provide information on how you provide value to your suppliers, customers and investors. This will be the key to sustaining your business relationships.

5. Meet and support your contacts

While the aforementioned methods are necessary, there is still no substitute to building and sustaining business relationships by meeting with them face-to-face over a coffee or lunch. Keep in touch with them regularly and find occasions to show appreciation for them.

If your contact invites you for a business event, show your support by bringing all of your employees to the event. This will leave a lasting impact on your contact, who may also find ways to support you more.
